Betrayals

Gaddari Korbe Madarchod

A backstabber is someone who smiles to our face, yet harbors ulterior motives behind our back. They feign friendship, pretend to be allies, and bask in our trust while secretly plotting against us. They are wolves in sheep's clothing, ready to pounce when we least expect it. Their actions leave scars, not only on individuals but also on the fabric of trust within our communities. The damage caused by a backstabber extends far beyond the immediate harm they inflict. Their deceit and betrayal ripple through our lives, creating a sense of vulnerability and suspicion in our interactions with others. Trust, the very foundation of any relationship, becomes fragile and difficult to rebuild once shattered.

Rahul Chaterjee
